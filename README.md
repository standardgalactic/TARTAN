[Fixing AI Hallucinations](https://standardgalactic.github.io/TARTAN/)  – *Audio Overview*

The **TARTAN architecture** proposes a shift in artificial intelligence from predictive approximation to a formal regime of **constraint closure**. Rather than treating observations as independent signals, it models them as **projections of a shared underlying state**, requiring reconstruction through global consistency rather than local fit.

Grounded in the **Relativistic Scalar–Vector Plenum (RSVP)** framework, inference is recast as the search for a configuration that simultaneously satisfies **projection constraints** and **dynamical admissibility conditions**. In this view, perception, reasoning, and modeling are unified as processes of **entropy reduction through structural reconciliation**.

The system establishes a correspondence between **variational consistency** and **sheaf-theoretic gluing**, where partial observations define local sections and inconsistencies manifest as **cohomological obstructions**. These obstructions are not treated as noise, but as structured indicators of incomplete or incompatible reconstruction.

To resolve these failures, TARTAN introduces a **staged closure protocol**, in which the system iteratively repairs inconsistent regions, refines resolution across ambiguous boundaries, or expands its representational capacity to incorporate previously unmodeled structure.

This process is governed by a **consistency operator**, whose fixed points correspond to globally admissible world-states. Under appropriate conditions of injectivity and convexity, this fixed point is unique, defining inference as convergence to a **single coherent reconstruction**.

The integrated **CLIO (Cognitive Loop via In-Situ Optimization)** module extends this framework by enabling recursive adaptation of the inference process itself. Rather than optimizing outputs, the system optimizes the **structure of its own reasoning**, embedding learning directly within the closure dynamics.

Within this framework, so-called “hallucinations” are reinterpreted as **failures of constraint satisfaction**—states that are locally plausible but globally inconsistent. Eliminating them is not a matter of filtering outputs, but of enforcing the conditions under which only **admissible configurations can persist**.

The accompanying visualization presents inference as a continuous dynamical system evolving over a constrained manifold. Geometry deforms under perturbation, while structural forces enforce coherence, illustrating the central principle:

**Knowledge is not prediction. It is the achievement of global consistency under constraint.**
